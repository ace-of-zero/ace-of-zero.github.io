Playing Statistics

Identifying the strongest gameplay strategies with machine learning.

Online card games like Hearthstone, Magic: The Gathering Arena, and Legends of Runeterra involve some very complex gameplay. Pro players and enthusiasts alike spend hours reviewing deck builds, win rates, and play strategies based on personal experience and publicly available records. This data is even more critical to the designers of such games. When I received the request to develop a dashboard to help a certain group of game designers make informed decisions when balancing gameplay, I knew I had to give it my all! After many hours of planning, learning, data cleaning and engineering, the final dashboard was received very well. One of its key features works kind of like this:

Strategy Name Popularity (% of Total Decks) Win Rate
Strategy 1 11.3% 48%
Strategy 2 9.34% 51%
Strategy 3 5.22% 42%

I fabricated this data for this example, but the data in the real McCoy comes from a live stream of game records. Each record includes a tokenized player ID, opponent ID, list of cards in the player's deck, and a few other details about the match. After cleaning and connecting this data stream to other necessary information, I set up an automated clustering algorithm in Python that identifies the top strategies that are being played based on the contents of each deck. These top strategies are given nicknames and assigned to each of the game records before being neatly wrapped back up into a series of visualizations. The example above shows how users may select a strategy of interest from the full list and have the chart react to show the strategy's popularity over time. The full product also includes a filterable matchup grid displaying 1v1 game outcome predictions, average decklists and card stats, and randomly sampled deck examples from each strategy.

The automation and python scripting behind this dashboard makes it my most complex business solution to date, and I have been thrilled with the feedback. The game designers have already asked for supplemental expansions to include additional game modes and adjacent gameplay insights!

Defective and Effective

Parsing millions of defect points to create executive-level reports

My career first started in the world of physical product engineering, but I very quickly learned that my passion truly lied within the analysis of the massive amounts of data generated throughout game production. This project started when the team I was working with requested a way to use customer complaint data to identify trends in product defects and factory performance. By working with the customer service team, I was able to establish a process by which product complaint data points (created when a customer reaches out about a defective product they received) are cleaned and stored in a SQL database. I combined this new data stream with financial data stored elsewhere on the database and illustrated our new trend-tracking capabilities with a dashboard featuring a chart that looked something like this:

The data is 100% fictional for this example, of course, but it recreates the aha! moment I felt when the data first confirmed for me a noticeable lag between orders and complaints, synchronized to the release dates of the biggest product releases.

My manager was thrilled with the possibilities of this new tool and requested a detailed weekly report that could be used to compare major product releases to eachother. This report is now shared weekly with business managers as a status update on product quality, and has grown considerably since its initial scope. Inspired by this success, I went on to create a new dashboard to present details related to individual product performance, which has successfully been used to identify trends within the defects themselves. The insights I have pulled from these dashboards has been used to initiate process improvements at factories to reduce defects at the source, as well as to improve upcoming products still in the development phase.

Side Gig - Art and AI

Learning and Illustrating With the Tools of the Future

In addition to the quantitative applications of machine learning and artificial intelligence, I am deeply and personally interested in the artistic side of the field. Cartoon artwork has been a cherished outlet for my imagination throughout my life, and when I first learned about Stable Diffusion, I was thrilled by the possibilities! Armed with a gaming PC, several insightful Github repos, and my own technical aptitude, I set up a local instance of the algorithm, configured web UI access on my iPad Pro, and began creating as man and machine. I didn't sleep for almost three nights in a row. Fully leaning into this frontier of study has benefitted my understanding of AI/ML greatly, as well. Inspired by the applications of generative art, I've also dipped my toes into open-source LLMs like Llama, Mistral, and Phi. While no open source model can wholistically outmatch GPT-4 yet, the application of a smaller LLM on a device like a Raspberry Pi is a thrilling possibility.
